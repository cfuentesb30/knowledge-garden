# Hindu Deity Map

## Purpose
This note is a quick deity-reference shelf for major Hindu divine forms and patterns.
Use it as an orientation tool, not as a claim that all Hindu traditions organize divinity the same way.

## Major deities and patterns
- **Vishnu** — preservation, order, avatars, protection
- **Krishna** — major Vishnu avatar in many traditions; devotion, play, divine teaching
- **Rama** — kingship, dharma, idealized heroism in the Ramayana
- **Shiva** — asceticism, destruction/transformation, dance, paradox, power
- **Devi** — divine feminine power in many forms
- **Durga** — protective warrior goddess
- **Kali** — fierce transformative goddess, time/death associations
- **Lakshmi** — prosperity, fortune, beauty
- **Saraswati** — learning, speech, arts
- **Parvati** — maternal and relational divine form tied to Shiva
- **Ganesha** — beginnings, thresholds, obstacle-removal
- **Hanuman** — devotion, loyalty, strength, service
- **Brahma** — creator figure in some theological schemata but less prominent in everyday worship than many others

## Important caution
Hindu divine life is not a simple fixed pantheon.
Different sects, texts, regions, and lineages rank or interpret divine forms differently.
