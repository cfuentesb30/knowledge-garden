# Egyptian Gods and Afterlife Map

## Purpose
This note is a quick-reference shelf for major Egyptian gods, key afterlife concepts, and funerary symbols.

## Major gods and divine powers
- **Ra / Re** — solar power, creation, kingship
- **Amun** — hiddenness, supremacy in some theological contexts
- **Osiris** — underworld, kingship after death, resurrection
- **Isis** — protective magic, motherhood, royal legitimacy
- **Horus** — royal power, sky, triumph over disorder
- **Set** — desert, violence, disorder, but also power and necessary force in some contexts
- **Anubis** — mummification, funerary guidance
- **Thoth** — writing, record, wisdom, calculation
- **Hathor** — joy, music, femininity, maternal and celestial dimensions
- **Sekhmet** — wrath, war, plague, healing force
- **Ptah** — craftsman/creator associations
- **Ma'at** — truth, order, justice personified

## Afterlife map
### Judgment
The dead are judged. The most famous image is the weighing of the heart against the feather of Ma'at.

### Osirian frame
Osiris becomes central to death, resurrection, and afterlife legitimacy.
To die well is often to enter an Osirian framework.

### Funerary preparation
- mummification
- tomb provisioning
- protective spells
- sacred texts
- names and images preserved for continuity

### Threats and protections
The afterlife is not passive drift. It requires guidance, protection, right speech, right preparation, and successful navigation.

## Important symbols
- feather of Ma'at
- scarab imagery
- solar barque
- ankh
- djed pillar
- eye symbolism in various forms

## Why it matters
Egyptian religion is one of the clearest cases where gods, kingship, ritual, and death are deeply integrated into one symbolic system.
