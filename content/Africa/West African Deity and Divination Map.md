# West African Deity and Divination Map

## Purpose
This note gives a quick reference for major divine and divinatory patterns in West African religious worlds.

## Major patterns
- high god / remote creator concepts in some traditions
- more active intermediary powers
- ancestor presence
- shrine-centered local powers
- divination as a major form of access to sacred order

## Yoruba-focused orientation
- **Olodumare** — high divine principle in many theological descriptions
- **Orunmila** — wisdom/divination associations via Ifa systems
- **Shango** — thunder, royal force, power
- **Oshun** — river, sweetness, fertility, attractiveness, diplomacy
- **Ogun** — iron, war, labor, tools, boundary-crossing force
- **Yemoja/Yemaya** — maternal/watery associations in various continuities

## Divination
**Ifa** is one of the most important reference systems here: not fortune-cookie superstition, but a complex body of sacred knowledge, interpretation, and ritual mediation.

## Why it matters
West African religions become much easier to understand once divination is seen as a core epistemic system, not a side feature.
