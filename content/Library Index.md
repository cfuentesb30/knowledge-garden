## How to use this library
- use regional overviews as entry points
- keep top-layer notes short and navigable
- put deep topic notes inside the relevant region folder
- use cross-regional notes for comparison, diffusion, syncretism, and thematic study
- use cross-regional indexes for fast retrieval of gods, rituals, sacred texts, festivals, and authority structures
- attach source and resource sections to deep notes whenever possible

## Current focus
- rebuild religion studies as a real library instead of a loose archive remnant
- prioritize historical depth, doctrinal structure, ritual practice, and regional development
- strengthen cross-regional comparison notes for power, sacrifice, text, ritual specialists, afterlife, revelation, purity, sacred law, mysticism, apocalypse, sacred objects, suffering, and coercion
- deepen the Europe shelf for esotericism, occultism, initiation systems, and cult/new religion analysis
- include trustworthy resource links for further study

## Suggested study dimensions
For each tradition or topic, try to cover:
- origins
- core beliefs
- ritual/practice
- institutions and clergy
- texts
- regional development
- interaction with politics/society
- syncretism or internal diversity
- recommended sources
