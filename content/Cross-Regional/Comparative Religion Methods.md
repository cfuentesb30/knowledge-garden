# Comparative Religion Methods

## What it is
This note explains how to compare religions without flattening them into clichés or forcing false equivalence.

## Core method
A good comparison asks:
- what problem is being compared?
- are the categories actually equivalent?
- is the comparison historical, theological, ritual, political, social, or symbolic?
- are we comparing elite doctrine, everyday practice, or institutional form?

## Common mistakes
- treating all religions as saying the same thing
- comparing surface similarities while ignoring structure
- projecting modern categories backward
- assuming one tradition is the default template

## Better comparison lenses
- cosmology
- ritual practice
- institution and authority
- sacred text and transmission
- salvation/liberation models
- relation to state and law
- views of the person, soul, and afterlife

## Recommended resources
- Jonathan Z. Smith
- Ninian Smart
- Wilfred Cantwell Smith
- university comparative religion syllabi

## Good working summary
Comparative religion is useful when it sharpens distinctions as much as similarities.
