# Sacred Geography and Pilgrimage

## Scope
This note compares how religions map holiness onto place: mountains, rivers, temples, shrines, tombs, cities, routes, and landscapes.

## Why it matters
Religions do not live only in beliefs or texts.
They live in geography.
Sacred space tells people where divine power is concentrated, where memory is anchored, and where ritual movement matters.

## Common patterns
- temple cities
- mountains as divine or liminal zones
- rivers as life, purity, fertility, or cosmic boundary
- tombs and relic sites
- pilgrimage routes
- sacred centers legitimizing political order

## Comparative examples
- Mecca and Hajj in Islam
- temple movement and sacred geography in ancient Egypt
- pilgrimage in Hindu traditions
- shrines in Shinto
- Jerusalem in Abrahamic traditions
- mountain and landscape devotion in Andean religion

## Why pilgrimage matters
Pilgrimage is not just travel.
It can be:
- devotion
- penance
- identity formation
- collective memory
- economic and political integration
- encounter with sacred power

## Good working summary
Sacred geography is one of religion's strongest material forms. It ties cosmology to land, movement, and memory.
