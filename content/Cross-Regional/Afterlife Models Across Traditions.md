# Afterlife Models Across Traditions

## Scope
This note compares different religious ways of imagining death, judgment, continuity, liberation, rebirth, heaven, underworlds, ancestors, and transformed existence.

## Why it matters
The afterlife is one of the clearest windows into how a religion understands:
- the self
- moral order
- cosmic justice
- ritual necessity
- salvation or liberation
- the relation between body, soul, memory, and destiny

## Major patterns
### Judgment-based models
The dead are assessed, weighed, judged, or sorted.
Examples include:
- Egyptian judgment imagery
- many Abrahamic afterlife frameworks

### Rebirth / reincarnation models
Death is not final sorting but transition into another life cycle.
Examples include many Hindu and Buddhist frameworks, though with important differences.

### Ancestor continuity models
The dead remain relationally active and socially important.
Examples appear strongly in many African, East Asian, and Indigenous traditions.

### Underworld journey models
The dead move through dangerous or structured postmortem realms requiring guidance, transformation, or ritual support.
Examples include Egyptian, Greco-Roman, and other ancient systems.

### Liberation/transcendence models
The goal is not merely a better afterlife location but release from delusion, attachment, or cyclic existence.
Examples include major Buddhist and Hindu philosophical/religious strands.

## Comparative questions
- what survives death?
- is judgment moral, ritual, cosmic, or all three?
- is the dead person's name/body/text memory important?
- do the living continue to support the dead?
- is the goal continuity, reward, purification, rebirth, or liberation?

## Why comparison helps
Comparing afterlife models reveals deep structural differences.
Some traditions want continuity of identity.
Some want justified transformation.
Some want reunion with God.
Some want release from the cycle entirely.

## Recommended resources
- Jan Assmann for Egypt
- standard introductions to Buddhist/Hindu/Abrahamic afterlife ideas
- anthropology of death and ancestor traditions

## Good working summary
Afterlife models are compressed maps of a religion's worldview. They reveal what counts as a person, what counts as justice, and what counts as ultimate success after death.
