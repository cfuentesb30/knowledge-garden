# Syncretism Overview

## What it is
Syncretism is the blending, adaptation, layering, or reinterpretation of religious traditions through contact, conquest, trade, migration, empire, or local continuity.

## Why it matters
A lot of religious history is not neat replacement. It is overlap, translation, survival, and fusion.

## Common conditions that produce syncretism
- conquest and colonial rule
- diaspora and displacement
- trade networks
- missionary translation
- intermarriage
- state pressure combined with local continuity

## Why the term needs caution
"Syncretism" can be useful, but it can also oversimplify.
Sometimes traditions are not blending evenly; one layer may be dominant while another survives underneath.

## Good working summary
Religious change is often additive, adaptive, and politically shaped, not just clean conversion from one system to another.
