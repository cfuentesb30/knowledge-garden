# Scripture Canon and Authority

## What it is
This note studies how religious communities decide which texts count, how those texts are interpreted, and who has authority over meaning.

## Core questions
- what counts as scripture?
- who decides canon?
- is authority textual, oral, institutional, charismatic, or distributed?
- how do interpretation disputes shape the tradition?

## Why it matters
Two traditions may both have "sacred texts" but operate very differently in how authority is assigned and contested.

## Study dimensions
- canon formation
- commentary traditions
- translation politics
- clergy/scholar authority
- oral transmission versus written fixation
- reform and heresy disputes

## Recommended resources
- Wilfred Cantwell Smith
- Jaroslav Pelikan
- primary-source introductions where possible
- Oxford / Cambridge companions for major traditions

## Good working summary
Sacred text alone does not explain a religion. Authority is shaped by the community, institutions, interpreters, and historical struggle around the text.
