# Festivals and Sacred Time

## Scope
This note compares how religious traditions organize sacred time through annual cycles, fasting periods, feast days, agricultural seasons, pilgrimage moments, and mythic commemorations.

## Why it matters
A religion is not just what it teaches. It is also how it structures time.
Sacred calendars organize memory, obligation, identity, and emotion.

## Common patterns
- annual festivals tied to agricultural cycles
- lunar or lunisolar calendars
- fasting periods
- feast days of saints or sacred events
- pilgrimage seasons
- initiation times
- new year cycles and cosmic renewal themes

## Comparative examples
- Ramadan and Eid cycles in Islam
- liturgical calendars and feast cycles in Christianity
- Saturnalia and civic festivals in Roman religion
- Panathenaia / Dionysia in Greek religion
- *matsuri* in Shinto contexts
- calendrical ritual in Mesoamerican religions

## Good working summary
Sacred time is one of the clearest ways religion enters ordinary life. It tells communities when to fast, celebrate, mourn, remember, and renew.
