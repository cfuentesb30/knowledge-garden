# Grimoires and Magical Manuals

## Scope
This note surveys grimoires and related magical manuals as textual technologies of ritual instruction, spiritual command, symbolic procedure, and occult transmission.

## Why it matters
If ceremonial magic is enacted structure, grimoires are often its handbook layer.
They preserve procedures, names, seals, invocations, and operational logic.

## What a grimoire is
A grimoire is generally a handbook of magical operations or ritual instructions.
It may include:
- conjurations
- seals and sigils
- planetary or angelic correspondences
- ritual timing
- spirit hierarchies
- protective procedures
- instructions for tools or sacred spaces

## Why they matter historically
Grimoires show that occultism was not only speculative philosophy.
It also had practical textual forms aimed at doing things.

## Important caution
Not every grimoire represents the same worldview.
Some are strongly Christian in frame.
Some are more explicitly magical-operational.
Some are compilations and textual patchworks.
Do not speak of "the grimoire tradition" as though it were one clean line.

## Why secrecy matters here
Grimoires are interesting because they preserve knowledge while also staging access.
Owning the text is not the same as mastering the practice.
Text, lineage, interpretation, and ritual competence still matter.

## Recommended resources
- Owen Davies
- Richard Kieckhefer

## Good working summary
Grimoires are ritual instruction books for magical practice. They turn secrecy, symbolism, and ritual procedure into textual form.
