# Ancient Greek Religion

## Scope
Ancient Greek religion was a polytheistic ritual system centered on gods, civic cult, sacrifice, festivals, oracles, and mythic tradition.

## Major gods
- **Zeus** — kingship, sky, thunder
- **Hera** — marriage, queenship
- **Athena** — wisdom, war strategy, civic order
- **Apollo** — music, prophecy, healing, plague, order
- **Artemis** — wilderness, hunt, childbirth
- **Ares** — war in its violent aspect
- **Aphrodite** — love, desire
- **Demeter** — agriculture and fertility
- **Dionysus** — wine, ecstasy, theater, altered states
- **Poseidon** — sea, earthquakes, horses
- **Hades** — underworld
- **Hermes** — boundaries, travel, trade, cunning, messages

## Ritual life
Greek religion was not mainly about doctrinal belief.
It centered on:
- sacrifice
- libations
- votive offerings
- festivals
- local cult sites
- temple maintenance
- processions
- mystery cult participation in some cases

## Important festivals and cultic dates
- **Panathenaia** — major Athenian festival for Athena
- **Dionysia** — linked to Dionysus and dramatic performance
- **Eleusinian Mysteries** — initiatory rites tied to Demeter and Persephone

## Religious features worth knowing
- city-specific cult emphasis mattered
- myth and ritual were linked but not identical
- oracles, especially Delphi, had major prestige
- gods were anthropomorphic but not morally simple
- mystery religions offered deeper initiation in some contexts

## Recommended resources
- Walter Burkert — *Greek Religion*
- Robert Parker — Athenian religion studies
- Oxford Classical Dictionary entries

## Good working summary
Greek religion was civic, ritual, local, mythic, and deeply woven into political and social life rather than confined to private belief.
