# Valentinianism

## Scope
Valentinianism is one of the most sophisticated and influential Christian-Gnostic currents of late antiquity, associated especially with Valentinus and later Valentinian schools.

## Why it matters
If people only know Gnosticism as bizarre cosmology, they miss the most intellectually serious parts.
Valentinianism matters because it shows a high-level attempt to interpret Christian revelation through a complex symbolic and cosmological system.

## Historical background
- Valentinus flourishes in the 2nd century CE
- associated with Christian intellectual circles in the Roman Empire
- later Valentinian branches and schools diversify

## Core themes
- emanational divine fullness
- aeons and layered spiritual structure
- rupture, deficiency, and restoration
- salvation through knowledge, revelation, and proper spiritual alignment
- strong engagement with Christian language and figures rather than simple rejection of them

## Why it is distinctive
Valentinianism often appears more intellectually integrated and scripturally engaged than the crude caricature of "Gnosticism" found in later polemic.
That is part of why it became such a serious opponent in heresiological literature.

## Authority and interpretation
Valentinians often competed not by denying Christianity outright but by offering deeper readings of Christian truth, scripture, and salvation.
That made the conflict partly one of interpretation and authority, not only belief labels.

## Why it matters for study
Valentinianism shows:
- that early Christianity had serious internal metaphysical competition
- that cosmology and scriptural interpretation were tightly linked
- that orthodoxy was consolidated through argument, exclusion, and institutional struggle

## Recommended resources
- Bentley Layton
- Karen King
- Elaine Pagels (usefully accessible, with supplementation)
- studies of Valentinian texts and late antique Christian diversity

## Good working summary
Valentinianism is one of the most important examples of Christian-Gnostic thought: sophisticated, scripturally engaged, cosmologically layered, and deeply relevant to the history of orthodoxy and heresy.
