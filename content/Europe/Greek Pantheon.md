# Greek Pantheon

## Purpose
This note serves as a deity-reference shelf for ancient Greek religion.
Use it alongside [[Ancient Greek Religion]] for quick lookup and later expansion into individual deity notes.

## Olympian and major gods
- **Zeus** — sky, thunder, kingship, oath-order
- **Hera** — marriage, queenship, legitimacy
- **Athena** — wisdom, strategy, crafts, civic intelligence
- **Apollo** — prophecy, music, healing, plague, solar associations in later reception
- **Artemis** — hunt, wilderness, liminality, childbirth protection
- **Ares** — violent war force
- **Aphrodite** — desire, beauty, erotic force
- **Poseidon** — sea, earthquake, horse power
- **Demeter** — grain, fertility, agricultural continuity
- **Dionysus** — wine, ecstasy, theater, boundary disruption
- **Hermes** — travel, trade, trickery, mediation, soul-guiding
- **Hades** — underworld sovereignty
- **Hestia** — hearth and domestic sacred stability

## Important patterns
- Greek gods are highly anthropomorphic but not morally simple
- local cult emphasis can matter more than textbook rank lists
- gods often overlap or shift in emphasis by region and period
- mythic role and cultic role are related but not always identical
