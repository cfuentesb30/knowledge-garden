# Alchemy Overview

## Scope
Alchemy is a complex family of practical, symbolic, cosmological, and transformative traditions dealing with substances, change, purification, hidden processes, and the relation between matter and spirit.

## Why it matters
Alchemy gets abused constantly in pop culture.
It is not just "trying to turn lead into gold" and it is not just metaphorical self-help either.
Historically, it sits in the overlap between craft practice, natural philosophy, symbolic cosmology, laboratory experimentation, and spiritual transformation.

## Major dimensions
### Material/laboratory dimension
Alchemy includes real work with substances, furnaces, vessels, metals, solvents, and transformation processes.

### Symbolic/cosmological dimension
Alchemy often treats material transformation as a window into hidden structures of reality.
Microcosm and macrocosm mirror one another.

### Spiritual/inner transformation dimension
In some traditions, outer work and inner refinement reflect each other.
Purification of matter and purification of the self become analogically linked.

## Historical range
Alchemy appears in multiple civilizations, but in the Western esoteric library here we focus mainly on Greco-Egyptian, Islamic, medieval, and early modern European transmission lines.

## Why Egypt matters
Egypt is not just background scenery here.
Alexandrian and Greco-Egyptian settings matter significantly in the development of alchemical language and authority traditions.

## Why alchemy matters historically
Alchemy matters because it sits near the transition zones between:
- religion
- esotericism
- craft knowledge
- medicine
- proto-chemistry
- symbolism
- secret transmission

## Common themes
- purification
- conjunction/opposition
- hidden essence
- transformation
- secrecy of process
- symbolic color stages in some traditions
- perfected matter and perfected operator in some systems

## Recommended resources
- Lawrence Principe — history of alchemy
- William R. Newman
- Stanton Linden
- scholarly work distinguishing practical alchemy from modern fantasy projection

## Good working summary
Alchemy is a historical transformation science and symbolism complex: part craft, part cosmology, part secrecy, part experiment, and sometimes part spiritual discipline.
