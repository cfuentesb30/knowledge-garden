# Rosicrucianism

## Scope
Rosicrucianism refers to an esoteric and reformist symbolic current emerging in early modern Europe, especially around a cluster of manifestos in the early 17th century, and later spawning numerous occult and initiatory reinterpretations.

## Why it matters
Rosicrucianism is one of the key bridges between:
- Renaissance esoteric thought
- reformist spiritual imagination
- secret society mythology
- later occult revival culture

## Historical core
The classical Rosicrucian moment is usually tied to early 17th-century German manifestos such as:
- *Fama Fraternitatis* (1614)
- *Confessio Fraternitatis* (1615)
- *Chymical Wedding of Christian Rosenkreutz* (1616)

## Key features
- hidden brotherhood motif
- reform of knowledge, religion, and society
- symbolic Christianity blended with esoteric and alchemical language
- prestige through secrecy or semi-secrecy
- mythic founder figure: **Christian Rosenkreutz**

## Why secrecy matters here
Rosicrucianism is as much about the idea of hidden enlightened reform as it is about any one concrete organization.
That is why it became so fertile for later myth-making.

## Historical importance
Rosicrucianism influenced:
- esoteric Christian speculation
- alchemical imagination
- occult fraternities
- later secret-order culture
- the symbolic prestige economy of hidden initiates guiding history from behind the curtain

## Important caution
Rosicrucianism is historically slippery.
There is a difference between:
- the original manifesto moment
- later Rosicrucian-inspired groups
- modern organizations using Rosicrucian branding

Do not collapse all three.

## Recommended resources
- Frances Yates (important but read critically)
- Christopher McIntosh
- Wouter Hanegraaff
- scholarship on early modern esotericism and manifesto culture

## Good working summary
Rosicrucianism is a mythically charged esoteric reform current built around hidden wisdom, symbolic Christianity, and the dream of enlightened transformation beneath visible institutions.
