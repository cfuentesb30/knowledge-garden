# Roman Religion

## Scope
Roman religion was a civic, legal, and ritual system focused on maintaining right relations between the human and divine orders through correct performance, public cult, household religion, and state ceremony.

## Core character
Roman religion was less about personal creed and more about:
- proper ritual performance
- public duty
- ancestral continuity
- state stability
- divine favor for the community

## Major gods
Some Roman gods were native in older form; others were identified with Greek gods over time.

- **Jupiter** — sky, sovereignty, state protection
- **Juno** — queenship, women, civic protection
- **Minerva** — wisdom, craft, strategy
- **Mars** — war, but also older agrarian/protective aspects
- **Venus** — love, beauty, lineage, political symbolism
- **Mercury** — commerce, movement, exchange
- **Neptune** — waters
- **Diana** — hunt, wilderness, moon associations
- **Vesta** — hearth, sacred civic continuity
- **Janus** — beginnings, thresholds, transitions
- **Saturn** — agriculture, deep antiquity, festival inversion through Saturnalia

## Domestic and civic religion
Roman religion worked on multiple levels:
- household cults
- local cults
- state cults
- military religion
- imperial cult in later periods

### Household religion
Important domestic divine figures included:
- **Lares** — guardian spirits linked to place/household
- **Penates** — household/storehouse protective powers
- ancestral memory and domestic offerings

## Ritual life
- sacrifice
- vows
- augury and omen reading
- priestly offices
- festival calendars
- processions
- temple maintenance
- state ceremonies tied to war, office, and agriculture

## Priesthood and offices
Roman religion was highly institutional.
It included:
- pontiffs
- augurs
- Vestal Virgins
- flamines
- state magistrates with ritual duties

## Important dates / festivals
- **Saturnalia** — December festival associated with Saturn, role inversion, gift-giving, loosened norms
- **Lupercalia** — February purification/fertility festival
- **Parilia** — pastoral purification festival
- many civic and temple calendars tied religion tightly to Roman public life

## Historical development
### Early Rome
Mixture of Italic, local, and gradually expanding cult systems.

### Republic
Religion deeply tied to public office, military legitimacy, and civic ritual.

### Empire
Imperial cult expands; syncretism increases; mystery cults and imported deities gain visibility.

## Recommended resources
- Mary Beard, John North, Simon Price — *Religions of Rome*
- Jörg Rüpke — Roman religion studies

## Good working summary
Roman religion was formal, civic, priestly, and politically embedded. It is best understood as a ritual system for maintaining order between gods, state, household, and empire.
