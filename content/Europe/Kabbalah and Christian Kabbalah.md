# Kabbalah and Christian Kabbalah

## Scope
This note distinguishes Jewish Kabbalah from Christian Kabbalah while explaining why both are so important to the history of esotericism.

## Why it matters
A lot of modern occult and esoteric material throws around "Kabbalah" carelessly.
That creates confusion fast.
Jewish Kabbalah is a real internal religious-intellectual tradition. Christian Kabbalah is a later reinterpretive appropriation and transformation.
They are connected, but they are not the same thing.

## Jewish Kabbalah
### Basic character
Kabbalah is a mystical and symbolic current within Judaism involving:
- divine emanation
- hidden meanings of scripture
- sacred language
- symbolic cosmology
- the relationship between the infinite and the structured world

### Common themes
- **Ein Sof** as divine infinity
- **sefirot** as structured emanational or symbolic dimensions
- scripture as layered text
- theurgic and contemplative possibilities in some interpretations

## Christian Kabbalah
### Basic character
Christian Kabbalah emerges when Christian thinkers adopt and reinterpret Kabbalistic materials in service of Christian theology, apologetics, and esoteric synthesis.

### Why it mattered historically
Christian Kabbalah became important in Renaissance esotericism because it seemed to offer:
- ancient hidden wisdom
- symbolic proof of Christian truths
- access to sacred linguistic and cosmological systems
- links between Judaism, Hermeticism, magic, and Christian reform thought

## Why the distinction matters
Jewish Kabbalah belongs inside Jewish religious history.
Christian Kabbalah is a translation, adaptation, and in some cases appropriation for other theological ends.
Confusing them erases the difference between source tradition and reinterpretive use.

## Esoteric importance
Christian Kabbalah later feeds into:
- ceremonial magic
- occult correspondences
- magical diagrams and symbolic systems
- Golden Dawn-style synthesis
- broader Western esotericism

## Recommended resources
- Gershom Scholem
- Moshe Idel
- Joseph Dan
- scholarship on Renaissance Christian Kabbalah

## Good working summary
Jewish Kabbalah is a Jewish mystical-symbolic tradition. Christian Kabbalah is a later esoteric Christian appropriation and reworking of Kabbalistic materials. Both matter, but for different reasons.
