# Europe Overview

## Scope
This section covers religious development in Europe from pre-Christian traditions through Christianity, heresy movements, esotericism, secularization, and modern revival currents.

## Why Europe matters religiously
Europe matters not because it invented religion, but because it became one of the major engines of:
- Christian institutional power
- church-state conflict and synthesis
- canon, heresy, reform, and confessional fragmentation
- esoteric reinterpretation and occult revival
- modern secularization and anti-clerical reaction
- the export of religious and anti-religious frameworks through empire

This shelf has to be read in relation to the Middle East and Mediterranean antiquity. Europe is a transformer and re-organizer, not the original source of everything it later centralizes.

## Major historical layers
### Ancient Mediterranean religion
Greek and Roman religion, civic cult, sacrifice, sacred kingship, mystery systems, and temple-public ritual life.

### Christian Europe
Catholic, Orthodox, and Protestant formations; monasticism; saints; relics; church-state negotiation; reform and schism.

### Heterodox and esoteric currents
Gnosticism reception, mystery legacies, Hermeticism, alchemy, Christian Kabbalah, Rosicrucianism, occult orders, magical systems, and modern revival movements.

### Modernity and reaction
Secularization, anti-clericalism, occult revival, romantic primitivism, reconstructed paganism, and the sociology of new religious movements.

## Core themes worth tracking
- civic religion and public cult
- orthodoxy vs heresy
- scripture and ecclesiastical authority
- sacrament, icon, relic, and sacred object controversies
- initiation, secrecy, and symbolic capital
- reform, fragmentation, and confessional violence
- occult revival as modern recombination rather than timeless continuity

## Research lenses that help here
- do not treat Europe as religious origin point by default
- distinguish ancient public cult, medieval Christianity, and modern occult revival
- pay attention to how institution, text, reform, and empire reshape religion
- separate internal mystical/esoteric traditions from later pop-occult kitsch
- watch how secrecy can function as pedagogy, prestige, or coercion

## Good working summary
Europe is a major religious re-organization zone: it inherits Mediterranean religions, centralizes Christianity, fractures into reform and confessional conflict, and later generates some of the most influential esoteric and secular reinterpretations of religion in the modern world.
