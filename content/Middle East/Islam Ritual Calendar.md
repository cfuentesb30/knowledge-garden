# Islam Ritual Calendar

## Purpose
This note gives a quick-reference map of major ritual times and observances in Islam.

## Calendar basis
Islam uses a lunar calendar.
That means major observances move through the solar year over long time spans.

## Major observances
### Ramadan
Month of fasting from dawn to sunset, increased prayer, charity, recitation, and communal religious intensity.

### Laylat al-Qadr
"Night of Power" associated with revelation, especially significant during Ramadan.

### Eid al-Fitr
Festival marking the end of Ramadan.

### Eid al-Adha
Festival associated with sacrifice and the Abraham/Ibrahim story, linked closely with the Hajj season.

### Hajj season
Pilgrimage cycle to Mecca with major ritual acts and sacred geography.

### Friday prayer
Weekly rather than annual, but highly important as the main congregational prayer setting.

## Why it matters
Islamic ritual time is not incidental. The calendar structures devotion, law, memory, and communal rhythm.
